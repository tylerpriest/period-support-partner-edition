# Content Strategy

## Tone & Voice

### Voice Characteristics

**Empathetic:** Understanding and validating
**Actionable:** Clear next steps, not just information
**Educational:** Teaching without lecturing
**Non-judgmental:** Safe space for learning
**Inclusive:** All genders, relationships, backgrounds
**Warm:** Approachable, not clinical
**Trustworthy:** Evidence-based, responsible

### What We Sound Like

**YES:**
- "Here's what can help during this phase..."
- "Many partners find it helpful to..."
- "This is a great time to..."
- "Consider checking in about..."

**NO:**
- "You must..." (commanding)
- "Obviously, you should..." (condescending)
- "Just do this..." (oversimplifying)
- "Are they on their period?" (dismissive)

---

## Content Hierarchy

### "Do This NOW" (Highest Priority)

**What:** Immediate, actionable guidance
**Where:** Dashboard, phase cards, quick actions
**Format:** 3-5 bullet points, scannable
**Examples:**
- "Have heating pad ready"
- "Ask: 'What can I do to make you comfortable?'"
- "Take on extra household tasks today"

**Design:** Large, prominent cards with clear CTAs

---

### "Learn More" (Secondary)

**What:** Deeper education, context, understanding
**Where:** Education tab, expandable sections
**Format:** Articles (500-1000 words), FAQs
**Topics:**
- How the menstrual cycle works
- Understanding PMS vs PMDD
- When to suggest seeing a doctor
- PCOS, endometriosis basics

**Design:** Browse-able library, progressive disclosure

---

### "Quick Reference" (Tertiary)

**What:** Communication guides, checklists
**Where:** Accessible from all screens, floating action button
**Format:** Do's/Don'ts lists, dialogue examples
**Examples:**
- "What to say during PMS"
- "Shopping list for period week"
- "Signs to watch for medical concern"

**Design:** Modal overlays, easy to dismiss and access

## Do's & Don'ts

### General Communication

**DO:**
- Ask what they need
- Validate their experience
- Be proactive (anticipate needs)
- Give space when requested
- Take notes on patterns
- Express care and concern
- Offer specific help

**DON'T:**
- Dismiss emotions as "just PMS"
- Make it about you
- Act squeamish
- Forget month after month
- Take mood personally
- Weaponize the cycle against them
- Make assumptions

### PMS-Specific

**DO:**
- Recognize emotions are amplified but real
- Be patient and understanding
- Reduce stress where possible
- Handle more tasks proactively
- Maintain stable, predictable presence

**DON'T:**
- Ask "are you on your period?" when upset
- Start arguments or serious talks
- Expect them to "snap out of it"
- Suggest they're being irrational
- Act like they're overreacting

## Inclusive Language

### Gender-Neutral Framing

- "Your partner" not "your girlfriend"
- "They/them" pronouns by default
- "Person experiencing period" not "woman"
- Customizable pronouns in settings

### Relationship Diversity

- No assumptions about relationship structure
- Inclusive of LGBTQ+ relationships
- Works for all genders as partners
- Polyamorous-friendly (if relevant)
