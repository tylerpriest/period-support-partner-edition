# User Needs & Feature Prioritization

## Target Users

### Primary User: The Partner (Supporting Role)
**Demographics:**
- Partners of all genders (men, women, non-binary)
- Age range: 18-50+ (focus on 20-40)
- Relationship types: Dating, committed, married, all orientations
- Tech comfort: Moderate to high (mobile app users)
- Health literacy: Variable (assume low to start)

**Psychographics:**
- Wants to be a better, more supportive partner
- May feel confused or helpless about menstrual cycle
- Willing to learn but intimidated by medical information
- Values the relationship and wants to reduce conflict
- May have grown up with stigma around periods
- Seeks practical, actionable guidance over theory

### Secondary Consideration: The Person Being Tracked
**Their Needs:**
- Control and consent over what's shared
- Trust that partner is using info appropriately
- Not wanting to feel "managed" or surveilled
- Appreciation for genuine support efforts

---

## Core User Jobs-to-be-Done

### Job 1: **"Help me understand what's happening"**
**When:** Partner notices mood changes, low energy, or physical symptoms
**Need:** Decode whether symptoms are cycle-related or something else
**Current Frustration:** "Is she upset with me or is it PMS?"

**Solution Requirements:**
- Clear phase indicator (where in cycle are we?)
- Common symptoms for this phase
- "Is this normal?" guidance
- Simple, non-medical language

---

### Job 2: **"Tell me what to do RIGHT NOW"**
**When:** Partner wants to help but doesn't know how
**Need:** Immediate, actionable support suggestions
**Current Frustration:** "I want to help but don't want to do the wrong thing"

**Solution Requirements:**
- Phase-specific action items
- Quick reference dos and don'ts
- Shopping lists (what to have on hand)
- Communication scripts

---

### Job 3: **"Prepare me for what's coming"**
**When:** Planning the week/month ahead
**Need:** Anticipate support needs
**Current Frustration:** "I keep being surprised even though this happens every month"

**Solution Requirements:**
- Cycle calendar and predictions
- Upcoming phase notifications
- Advance warnings for PMS window
- Planning suggestions (when to have important talks, when to plan dates)

---

### Job 4: **"Educate me without making me feel dumb"**
**When:** Partner wants to learn about menstrual health
**Need:** Build knowledge and empathy
**Current Frustration:** "I'm embarrassed to ask basic questions"

**Solution Requirements:**
- Judgment-free learning environment
- Progressive education (basics to advanced)
- "No stupid questions" approach
- Clear, simple explanations

---

### Job 5: **"Help me communicate better"**
**When:** During tense moments or vulnerable phases
**Need:** Navigate emotionally charged conversations
**Current Frustration:** "Everything I say during PMS makes it worse"

**Solution Requirements:**
- What to say / what NOT to say
- Understanding emotional amplification vs. real issues
- Timing guidance for serious conversations
- Validation and empathy scripts

---

### Job 6: **"Make me feel capable and confident"**
**When:** Ongoing relationship maintenance
**Need:** Build partner's sense of competence
**Current Frustration:** "I feel helpless and like I'm walking on eggshells"

**Solution Requirements:**
- Positive reinforcement
- Quick wins (easy actions that make a difference)
- Progress tracking (how am I doing?)
- Success stories

## Feature Prioritization Framework

### MoSCoW Analysis

## **MUST HAVE (MVP - Minimum Viable Product)**

### 1. **Cycle Tracking & Phase Display**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- Input/sync cycle start date
- Calculate current phase (menstrual, follicular, ovulation, luteal)
- Visual indicator of where in cycle
- Days until next period
- Simple, glanceable dashboard

**Why Critical:** This is the foundational feature - without knowing where in the cycle, nothing else works.

---

### 2. **Phase-Specific Quick Actions**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- 3-5 immediate action suggestions per phase
- Categorized: Physical Support / Emotional Support / What to Say
- Quick-scan card format
- Update based on current phase

**Why Critical:** This is the core value - actionable guidance partners desperately want.

---

### 3. **Communication Do's & Don'ts**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- Per-phase communication guide
- What TO say examples
- What NOT to say examples
- Quick reference format

**Why Critical:** Communication mistakes cause the most relationship damage. This prevents harm and builds connection.

---

### 4. **Onboarding Flow**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- Welcome and value proposition
- Consent framework (both partners agree)
- Basic cycle education (101 level)
- Initial cycle data input
- Customization (partner's specific symptoms/preferences)

**Why Critical:** First impressions matter. Must make partner feel welcome and capable immediately.

---

### 5. **Notifications/Reminders**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- Period starting soon (2-3 days ahead)
- PMS window beginning (3-5 days before period)
- Phase transitions
- Customizable - partner chooses what they want

**Why Critical:** Proactive reminders enable anticipatory support, which is much more appreciated than reactive.

---

### 6. **Privacy & Consent Settings**
**Priority:** CRITICAL
- Clear data sharing agreement
- Revoke access anytime
- What data is shared (transparent)
- Local data storage option

**Why Critical:** Ethical requirement. Without proper consent framework, app is creepy and potentially harmful.

---

## **SHOULD HAVE (High Value, Phase 2)**

### 7. **Educational Content Library**
**Priority:** HIGH
- Cycle basics (how it works)
- Hormone explanations
- Common conditions (PCOS, endometriosis, PMDD)
- Pain management strategies
- Relationship impact

**Format:** Progressive disclosure
- Quick 2-min reads
- Deeper dives available
- Video content (optional)
- Interactive quizzes

**Why High Priority:** Builds long-term understanding and empathy, but partners need action items first.

---

### 8. **Symptom Tracker & Notes**
**Priority:** HIGH
- Log observed symptoms
- Add notes ("seemed stressed about work" vs. "PMS")
- Pattern recognition over time
- Share notes with partner (optional)

**Why High Priority:** Helps differentiate cycle-related from situational, builds attunement.

---

### 9. **Customizable Phase Lengths**
**Priority:** HIGH
- Adjust average cycle length
- Customize phase durations
- Irregular cycle support
- Past cycle history improves predictions

**Why High Priority:** Everyone's cycle is different - personalization improves accuracy and relevance.

---

### 10. **Shopping List / Prep Reminders**
**Priority:** HIGH
- "Stock up now" lists before period
- Preferred products
- Comfort items
- Medication reminders

**Why High Priority:** Practical, tangible support. Partners love checklists.

---

### 11. **Relationship Insights**
**Priority:** HIGH
- "Best time to plan date night" (follicular/ovulation)
- "Save serious talks for next week" (during PMS)
- "Extra patience needed this week"
- Calendar integration

**Why High Priority:** Unique differentiation from other apps. Focuses on relationship health, not just biology.

---

### 12. **Daily/Weekly Tips**
**Priority:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- Rotating supportive content
- Mood/energy predictions
- Small, actionable daily suggestions
- Encouragement and validation

**Why Medium-High:** Drives daily engagement (DAU metric), builds habit.

---

## **COULD HAVE (Nice to Have, Future Phases)**

### 13. **Community/Forum**
**Priority:** MEDIUM
- Partner-to-partner support
- Ask questions anonymously
- Share success stories
- Moderated for safety

**Why Medium:** Builds loyalty and engagement, but resource-intensive to moderate.

---

### 14. **Partner Progress Tracking**
**Priority:** MEDIUM
- Completed educational modules
- "Supportive actions" logged
- Badges/gamification (light)
- Share progress with partner (optional)

**Why Medium:** Gamification can drive engagement, but risks feeling performative.

---

### 15. **Mood/Energy Predictions**
**Priority:** MEDIUM
- AI/ML predictions based on past cycles
- "Expect lower energy this week"
- Confidence levels in predictions

**Why Medium:** Adds sophistication, but requires data and algorithms MVP doesn't need.

---

### 16. **Integration with Partner's Tracker**
**Priority:** MEDIUM
- Sync with Flo, Clue, or other tracking apps
- Import cycle data directly
- Bidirectional updates

**Why Medium:** Technical complexity, but valuable for couples already using other apps.

---

### 17. **Multi-Language Support**
**Priority:** LOW-MEDIUM
- Localization for major languages
- Cultural customization

**Why Low-Medium:** Start with English, expand based on traction.

---

### 18. **Voice/Chat Interface**
**Priority:** LOW
- "Hey app, what should I do today?"
- Quick Q&A without navigating UI

**Why Low:** Cool but not essential. Complex to build well.

---

### 19. **Partner Wellness Tracking**
**Priority:** LOW
- Track partner's own stress, sleep, health
- Understand mutual influence

**Why Low:** Scope creep - stay focused on cycle support first.

## User Flow Priorities

### First-Time User (Day 1)
1. Welcome screen (what is this app?)
2. Consent and privacy explanation
3. Basic cycle education (2-3 screens)
4. Input cycle data (or sync)
5. Customize preferences
6. **First win:** See current phase + 3 action items
7. Set up notifications

**Success Metric:** User completes onboarding and sees immediate value in <5 minutes

---

### Daily Engaged User
1. Open app
2. Glance at dashboard: current phase, days to next period
3. See 3-5 action items for today
4. Maybe read a daily tip
5. Done in <30 seconds

**Success Metric:** Daily active user can get value in <1 minute

---

### Learning User (Weekly Engagement)
1. Open app
2. Current phase overview
3. Browse educational content
4. Complete quiz or module
5. Adjust settings based on learning

**Success Metric:** Weekly engagement with educational content

---

### Crisis User (PMS Window)
1. Open app during tense moment
2. See "PMS Window" indicator
3. Quick access to communication dos/don'ts
4. De-escalate situation with guidance
5. Feel validated and capable

**Success Metric:** App helps during challenging moments, builds trust

## Engagement & Retention Hooks

### Daily Return Triggers
- **Push notifications**: Phase changes, upcoming period, daily tips
- **Streak system**: Days of checking in or supportive actions
- **Daily randomized tip**: Something new each day
- **Widget**: Glanceable info without opening app

### Weekly Return Triggers
- **Educational module unlocks**: Progressive learning path
- **Relationship insights**: "This week's focus"
- **Pattern insights**: "Here's what we learned this month"

### Monthly Return Triggers
- **Cycle recap**: How did the month go?
- **Relationship health score** (optional, non-judgmental)
- **Content refresh**: New articles, updated guidance

## Platform Considerations

### Mobile-First (iOS + Android)
**Why:** Partners need info on-the-go, daily engagement, push notifications essential

### Notification Strategy
- **Opt-in, customizable**: Never spammy
- **High value only**: Phase changes, period coming, PMS window
- **Timing**: Morning (prepare for day) or customizable
- **Tone**: Supportive, not alarming

### Offline Capability
- **Core features work offline**: View current phase, action items, saved content
- **Sync when online**: Update predictions, new content

## Key Insights

1. **Simplicity over Complexity**: Partners don't want to become medical experts. They want quick, clear actions.

2. **Action over Information**: "Do THIS" beats "Here's why" every time for primary use case.

3. **Anticipation is Key**: Knowing what's coming is more valuable than reacting to what's happening.

4. **Communication is Make-or-Break**: Wrong words during PMS can tank a relationship. Right words build intimacy.

5. **Quick Wins Build Trust**: Partners need to feel capable and successful immediately, not after weeks of learning.

6. **Consent is Non-Negotiable**: Without ethical framework, app feels like surveillance tool.

7. **Inclusivity Expands Market**: LGBTQ+ community is underserved and loyal when included.

8. **Relationship Focus = Differentiation**: Other apps track health. We build relationships.
